Books commonly banned in public school libraries

Melissa by Alex Gino

  Melissa has been challenged in libraries for having a 10 year old transgender protagonist. Many parents feel that their children should not be learning about gender and sexuality at such a young age.
  "Several patrons of the Lincoln Parish Library in Ruston (LA) complained about George and Rick, two books in the children's room collection featuring LGBTQIA+ characters. The identical complaints referenced an upcoming mill levy election for the library. While the patrons agreed that neither book was sexually graphic, they expressed concern that children could encounter a topic the parents didn't want them to."
  The book was originally titled George, but Gino realized that "The title of [the] book made it seem as though it is OK to use an old name for a person when they have provided you with a different name that works better for them." The title was changed to Melissa to reflect these thoughts.
  Due to these factors, Melissa has remained a controversial book in elementary school libraries.

This Book is Gay by Juno Dawson

  As the title suggests, This Book is Gay revolves around sexual education for queer identifying young adults. Despite being targeted away from, the book was banned for containing sexual imagery, under the argument that "books like [This Book is Gay] are 'obscene and pornographic' — as Henry McMaster, the Republican governor of South Carolina put it — and it is even suggested that authors are 'grooming' young people for deviant lifestyles."
  Dawson, the author of the challenged book, wrote an article about having written such a crittically acclaimed yet controversial book.

Fighting Back: The Lawsuit Against Escambia County

  Ulaby states, "Escambia County in northwest Florida has removed or restricted at least 16 books from public school libraries and classrooms."
