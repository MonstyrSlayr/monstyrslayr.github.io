Do students feel safe in their schools?

What is safety?

Safety is a welcoming, diverse, adaptive environment with a variety of educational opportunities, student downtime, and adults who can act as mentors.

What does it mean for a student to be unsafe?

A student can feel unsafe inside and outside of school. Factors like homelessness and poverty are major contributors to students feeling unsafe outside of schools. The student is more concerned with their financial situation than with learning. Other factors outside of schools are family drug abuse and violent home lives.

Henry Clay High School

  Despite seemingly extensive safety measures, a student was able to bring a loaded gun into Henry Clay High School. How was this possible?
  Henry Clay High School is an American public school located in Kentucky.
  As it turned out, the safety measures were not as thorough as they seemed on the surface. At the school, when a student walked into the building, their bag would be searched by security to make sure the school remained safe. However, according to a student at Henry Clay High School, "some of them search the bags pretty well and then other ones look at them and they don't really care. Like they'll just open one pocket."
