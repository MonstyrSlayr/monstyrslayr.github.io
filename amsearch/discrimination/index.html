Discrimination in Public Schools

What types of discrimination are present in schools?

Racism

Racism is discrimination against an individual due to their race, which is mostly determined by skin color and ethnicity. Research shows that this type of discrimination can show up as early as preschool.

Misogyny

Misogyny is discrimination against women. etc etc

Homophobia

Homophobia is discrimination against homosexual people.

Transphobia

Transphobia is discrimination against transgender people.

Ableism

Ableism is discrimination in favor of able-bodied people.

Yale Research on Implicit Bias

  Researchers at Yale found out that implicit bias in students can begin as early as preschool. Yale researchers conducted a study in which teachers were instructed to look at preschool students with the intent of finding challenging behaviors. The results they drew were astounding.
  "Findings revealed that when expecting challenging behaviors teachers gazed longer at Black children, especially Black boys. Findings also suggested that implicit biases may differ depending on teacher race. Providing family background information resulted in lowered severity ratings when teacher and child race matched, but resulted in increased severity ratings when their race did not match." - from a brief from the Yale Child Study Center.

  This study proved that racial discrimination is an issue even in the early stages of school. But what exactly is implicit bias? Implicit bias is bias that occurs automatically and accidentally. The teachers in the study were unaware that they were being studied for implicit biases, which made the study more reliable and genuine - and significant.
  "Black preschoolers are 3.6 times as likely to receive one or more suspensions relative to White preschoolers. This is particularly concerning as Black children make up only 19% of preschool enrollment, but comprise 47% of preschoolers suspended one or more times."
  This is especially concerning, because it means behaviors seen in black preschoolers can be seen as condemning while the same behaviors seen in white preschoolers can be seen as normal.

Lawsuit Against the Oakland School District

  Up until 2012, schools in the Oakland District were suspending black boys at a significantly higher rate than white boys.
  "According to figures obtained by the Los Angeles Times, African-Americans accounted for 63 percent of students in the district with at least one suspension and 61 percent of those who were expelled, but make-up just 39 percent of the district's total enrollment."
  As you can imagine, this issue is rooted in racism and has been investigated by federal monitors.
